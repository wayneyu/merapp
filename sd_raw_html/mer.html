Solution
To find the equation of the tangent line to $y = x^{3.5} - e^{3.5}$ at the point $(e, 0)$, we must first find the derivative of the curve, which is the slope of the tangent line at that point. First we use the difference rule to separate the two terms. $ \frac{d}{dx}(x^{3.5}-e^{3.5}) =\frac{d}{dx}(x^{3.5})-\frac{d}{dx}(e^{3.5}) $ The next step is to recognize that $e^{3.5}$ is a constant, so we know that its derivative is equal to 0 and we can remove that term altogether from the equation. Then we use to power rule to differentiate $x^{3.5}$: $ \frac{d}{dx}(x^{3.5}) =3.5x^{3.5-1} =3.5x^{2.5} $ By plugging in x = e into the derivative, we find the tangent line at the desired point $(e, 0)$, we get the slope $m$ of the tangent line of equation $y-y_{0} = m(x - x_{0})$ \( \displaystyle m=3.5(e^{2.5}) \) From this we can conclude that the equation of the line is: $ \displaystyle y-y_0=m(x-x_0) \quad\Rightarrow\quad y-0=3.5e^{2.5}(x-e) $ Simplifying, we get $ \displaystyle y=3.5e^{2.5}(x-e) $ or \( \displaystyle y=3.5e^{2.5}x-3.5e^{3.5} \)
